Projects

P-AgBot

I worked in Purdue's Multi-Scale Robotics and Automation Lab to make a new configuration of P-AgBot, one designed for the autonomous deployment and monitoring of soil moisture sensors developed by the Internet of Things for Precision Agriculture (IoT4Ag) at the University of Pennsylvania. A robust mechanical and electrical system was created and a deep learning-based visual servoing method was developed for motion control. Look out for a paper that will be published at the start of 2026!

#AutonomousRobots #PrecisionAgriculture #SensorDeployment #ComputerVision #DeepLearning

Search and Rescue Robot (SARR)

In MAE 322: Mechanical Design, I was apart of the team Doofenshmirtz Evil Inc. and designed a Search and Rescue Robot for the traversal of rough terrain, including a 8 foot long ramp, dogleg chute, and a 1 foot high wall, and the autonomous delivery of a 1 kilogram med kit. I was the team lead for the med kit delivery system and worked on every aspect of this robot. My team successfully navigated the whole course and delivered the med kit autonomously.

#SearchAndRescueRobotics #AutonomousRobots #DifferentialSteering #ObstacleNavigation

Optimized Airplane Wing

In MAE 321: Engineering Design, I was a part of the team that designed an optimized airplane wing. Within my team, I helped lead the CAD design in CREO Parametric. Our wing is primarily comprised of a specially designed I-beam and 8 bulkheads. During testing, our 1.33lb beam withstood 245lbs before breaking the test apparatus at 270lbs.

#Aircraft Structures #CREOParametric #Optimization

Wind Turbine Blades

In MAE 224: Integrated Eng. Science Laboratory, I worked in a small team to design and perform an experiment analyzing the relation between the coefficient of performance of wind turbines and the number of turbine blades. This experiment was performed in a small wind tunnel using different 3d printed turbine blades.

#WindEnergy #TurbineBladeDesign #RenewableEnergy

Self-Balancing Inverted Pendulum

In MAE 433: Automatic Control Systems, I designed a control scheme to create a self-balancing pendulum.

#ControlSystems #DynamicStability

1954 Triumph Tiger Cub Restoration

In FRS 106: Art and Science of Motorcycle Design, I restored a 1954 Triumph Tigercub to working condition. In this project, I led the team in charge of the restoration of the bottom end of the engine. This specific portion of the project required a lot of cleaning and required me to design a way to take use an old scanner to make vectorized images for making custom gaskets. This method was documented in an internal guide to allow future classes to replicate this process. The whole project is documented on this website . This project also inspired me to get my motorcycle license.

#Motorcycles #Gaskets #Restoration

Metadata Processing for Princeton Latin American Ephemera Collection

For the past 3 years, I've been one of the main contributors to Princeton's Digital Archive of Latin American and Caribbean Ephemera. As of summer 2025, I have processed 2033 unique pieces of ephemera. This processing requires spanish and portuguese reading comprehension.
